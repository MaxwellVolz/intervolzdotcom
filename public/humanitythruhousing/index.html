Humanity Thru Housing is developing a network of Living Hubs designed to provide stable, resilient, and self-sustaining communities for displaced individuals and families. Our approach goes far beyond traditional shelter models. It is a fully integrated operating ecosystem, engineered with modular design principles, systems-level thinking, and long-range maintenance planning.

How It Works

Each hub is built on a foundation of resilience—balancing energy loads, optimizing water systems, and supporting community life through infrastructure that is both durable and adaptable. This is not simply a construction initiative; it is an engineered environment capable of supporting people, services, and long-term recovery.

Core Components of a Standard Living Hub

50 Housing Units

Modular, engineered living spaces designed for durability, comfort, and rapid deployment.

Medical Center

Comprehensive healthcare facility with pharmacy and education center for community wellness.

Canteen & Training Center

Community dining and workforce development hub for skills training and job readiness.

Agriculture Center

On-site food production and therapeutic horticulture for sustainability and community engagement.

Operating Eco System (OES)

The backbone of every Living Hub, providing essential infrastructure and services for the entire complex through an integrated, engineered system.

Power Generation

Complete electrical infrastructure for the entire complex

Water Filtration

Advanced water purification and distribution systems

Black-Water Treatment

Collection and treatment of wastewater

Internet Service

Connectivity infrastructure for residents and services

Security

Comprehensive safety and monitoring systems

Program Overview

A $40M initiative to build 10 self-sustaining living hubs across the Western United States, providing transitional housing for 2,000 veterans, displaced workers, and those experiencing homelessness.
