# How I Work

This article is inspired by a series of articles on [lifehacker](www.lifehacker.com). Specifically, [this one about Tim Ferris](https://lifehacker.com/im-tim-ferriss-and-this-is-how-i-work-5961603). Wow. Published in 2012. Feeling old.

Shamelessly going to use the intro as a template. Feel free to tell me not to do so.

- **Name**: Maxwell Volz
- **Occupation**: Software Engineer. Currently automating lab instruments and creating holistic solutions for performance testing of cutting edge hardware prototypes in Meta's ARVR division
- **Location**: San Francisco, CA. I came for an internship in 2012, realized the dream, and haven't left
- **Current daily OS(s)**: Windows11, MacOS, Ubuntu24
- **Current mobile devices**: iPhone 14 Pro

## Programming

- **IDE**: VS Code, notable extensions: black formatter (py), Markdown All in One, Markdown Table
- **Shell**: zsh with oh my zsh!

## Art & Assets

- **Vectors**: Adobe Illustrator, long time fan and user and unable to replace due to hotkeys and workflow
- **Pixels**: Adobe Photoshop, see ^
- **Diagrams**: [apps.diagrams.net](https://app.diagrams.net/)
- **QuickSnips**: `Win + Shift + S` and `Paint` or `Cmd + Shift + 4` or `Cmd + 4`
- **Blender**: I'm slow! And not great! But learning via Youtube and should have a cool update on this soon...

## Computer Peripherals

- **Keyboard**: KeyChron K4 but I really want a `Keychron Q3 Max`
- **Mouse**: Zowie FK1+
- **Additional**: StreamDeck, nice for quick discord mute/deafen, spotify controls, need to integrate more

## AI Integration

- **Browser**: ChatGPT Pro
- **Other**: Currently none, I've tried CodePilot and a few others but felt more like it was an annoyance. Looking to improve...

## What's apps/software/tools can't you live without?

The Adobe Suite.
Discord. Used to be Ventrilo, I miss those days.

## What's your workspace like?

My desk is usually cluttered but my windows are very organized. I use hotkeys with the numpad on MacOS to position terminals efficiently.

Spotify. My mix is rather eclectic. Lately it's been one of 3 radio stations made from the following:

## What's your best time-saving trick?

Just getting started. When the inspiration strikes the quicker I can get my fingers on a keyboard or a pen and a pad.


## What's your favorite to-do list manager?

[Notion.so](www.notion.so). Discovered it via [hackernews](www.ycombinator.com/news) almost a year ago and we are all in.

Previously I was managing notes in markdown format with lots of elbow grease and a wild folder structure. Happy to be past that.

## Besides your phone and computer, what gadget can't you live without?

My watch. A classic Casio. My 8th grade Algebra teacher once made a remark about how important men wear watches because they have things to do. Weird and clear memory. Eh. Maybe I like the idea that I am now that man, wearing a watch, being important.

## What everyday thing are you better at than anyone else? What's your secret?

Breadth of knowledge from general to technical computing, mostly web and application-based functionality, but modern technology has moved most computing interactions to under these umbrellas. I bring a lot of original ideas to the table due to my unconventional background and desire to create. I am a hacker and a gamer.

## What's the best advice you've ever received?
