How It Works

Self-sustaining communities built on patented pre-fabricated construction, RV permitting for fast-track siting, and partnerships with local governments and ecumenical organizations.

Core Infrastructure

Pre-Fabricated Units

Patented, RV-rated construction for rapid permitting and easy relocation. 200 units per hub with low-cost manufacturing.

Central Service Unit (CSU)

Off-grid power generation (74kW solar + diesel backup), water filtration, communications, and security—all remote-monitored.

On-Site Services

Job Training

Skills development and workforce readiness programs

Counseling Services

Mental health support and case management

Cultural Arts Center

Community engagement and creative programming

Canteen

On-site dining and community gathering space

Community Gardens

Fresh food access and therapeutic horticulture

RV Permitting

Fast-track siting in under 30 days through DOT partnerships in CA, NV, OR, and AZ

Easy Relocation

Ability to relocate entire communities for lower-cost property acquisition

For-Profit Model

Sustainable business approach with government and faith-based partnerships

Program Overview

A $40M initiative to build 10 self-sustaining living hubs across the Western United States, providing transitional housing for 2,000 veterans, displaced workers, and those experiencing homelessness.
