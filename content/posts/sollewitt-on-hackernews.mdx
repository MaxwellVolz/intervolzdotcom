What happens when your side project hits the front page of Hacker News.

### tl;dr

- **43 points** on HN translated to **~2,000 unique human visitors** and **17,948 hits** on the project page
- The traffic spike lasted **~10 hours** at sustained levels before tapering
- A static site on a home Intel NUC behind Cloudflare handled the entire HN front page load with **zero errors**
- The post rippled out to **9+ aggregator/social platforms** beyond HN itself
- Distinctly technical crowd with **macOS + Linux users making up 43%** of the audience
- Bandwidth was trivial for even a home connection peaking at ~57 MB in a single hour

## February 2nd at Noon

I submitted [the Sol LeWitt generator](https://intervolz.com/sollewitt) to Hackernews and it didn't completely flop!

| Metric | Value                                                                                  |
| ------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Title  | *Show HN: Sol LeWitt-style instruction-based drawings in the browser*                  |
| Link   | [news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46965968](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46965968) |

## Hour One

Not even an upvote. I was plummetting to the bottom of the stack.

~220 hits on my site. **Zero upvotes**. 

The post was sitting somewhere on page 5 of `/new`. 

I checked a few times, saw it hadn't moved, and figured that was it. I sent a text that said "nobody likes me" *lol*.

I know most HN submissions die a short death. That's just how it works.

## Getting Traction

I had my `GoAccess` logs pulled up on a second monitor to monitor the web traffic in real-time and `Unique Visitors` was crawling...

Then it started picking up! A few upvotes and bang! Page 2.

And then it was on the front page. Sitting right there between the usual mix of Show HNs and tech articles.

Stoked. I've been reading articles and checking links at this exact location for a decade.

![Hacker News front page](/imgs/sol_hn_frontpage.jpeg "On the front page!")

It peaked at **#16**. Not the top of the page, but *on the page*. For a solo side project I built over a weekend, that felt pretty good.

The spike was sharp. A big burst over a few hours, a long tail through the rest of the day, and trending back to normal now.

![GoAccess traffic overview](/imgs/sol_hn_goaccess.jpeg "Midnight, 12 hours since posted")

## What I Learned

**Metadata matters more than you think.** I love HN because of its lack of fluff. Just a text link, no previews, no images. But there's a whole ecosystem of sites that mirror or enhance HN content: [hckrnews.com](https://hckrnews.com), [hnews.xyz](https://hnews.xyz), various RSS readers, and social media bots that auto-post trending stories.

![hckrnews.com](/imgs/sol_hn_hckrnews.png "How the post looked on hckrnews.com")

These sites *do* pull metadata. Open Graph images, descriptions, page titles, etc. If your page has a good `og:image` and a clear `og:description`, your post looks significantly better on these aggregators compared to a bare link with no preview. It's the difference between someone scrolling past and someone clicking through.

### Why It Survived the Hug of Death

1. **Fully static:** No database, no server-side rendering, no API calls. Just HTML, JS, CSS, and fonts.
2. **Cloudflare caching:** CDN absorbed repeat requests; only ~12% of requests hit origin with 304s.
3. **Canvas 2D rendering is client-side:** The expensive generative art computation happens in each visitor's browser, not on the server.
4. **Small payload:** Full page load ~170KB (compressed). The NUC never broke a sweat.
5. **Zero downtime:** 0 `5xx` errors on /sollewitt paths. Every single request was served successfully.
